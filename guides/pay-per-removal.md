# Pay per removal, not per month

Most DMCA takedown services bill a monthly retainer whether or not anything comes down. [Delister](https://delister.co) charges per course, on success: you pay $79 when your course is removed, and a removal counts only when the listing is de-indexed from Google Search or the file is taken down at the host. If nothing comes down, there is nothing to pay.

## Why per-removal instead of a retainer

A retainer charges you for activity: notices sent, dashboards maintained, hours logged. None of that is the thing you want, which is your paid course gone from the free-download results that compete with your sales page. Per-removal pricing puts the risk on the service: the fee is earned only when the outcome lands, so both sides want the same thing.

## What counts as a removal

Two outcomes count, and both are ones you can verify yourself:

- **De-indexed from Google Search.** The listing no longer appears when you search your course name plus terms like "free download." Almost all traffic to a pirated listing arrives this way, so removing it from results stops the harm even if the page still exists somewhere.
- **Removed at the host.** The file no longer downloads: the host has taken it down at the source, which kills every listing that pointed at it. This is the stronger outcome and the one to aim for first when the file sits on a compliant host.
